## Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our revenue management services.

1\. What is hotel revenue management?

Hotel revenue management is a data-driven strategy to sell the right room to the right guest at the right time for the right price. It involves analyzing market demand, competitor pricing, booking patterns, and historical data to optimize your room rates and maximize revenue. Rather than using fixed pricing, revenue management uses dynamic pricing that adjusts based on demand—similar to how airlines price tickets. The goal is to increase your overall revenue and profitability while maintaining healthy occupancy levels.

2.How much does revenue management cost?

Revenue management services typically work on a performance-based model, often charging a percentage of revenue increase (usually 4-8%) or a flat monthly fee based on property size. Many providers offer flexible pricing structures including commission-based (you only pay when revenue increases), monthly retainer, or hybrid models. The investment typically pays for itself many times over through increased revenue. For a 50-room hotel, you might invest $1,500-$3,000 per month, but see $10,000-$20,000 in additional monthly revenue. We offer customized pricing based on your property size and needs—contact us for a detailed quote.

3.Can I still offer discounts and promotions?

Yes, absolutely! Revenue management doesn't eliminate discounts—it makes them strategic. We help you determine when, where, and how much to discount to maximize impact. Instead of blanket discounts that erode profit, we use strategic promotions targeted at the right times (low-demand periods), right channels (direct bookings vs OTAs), and right guest segments (corporate, leisure, extended stay). We'll also help you create packages that add value without reducing rates, and implement rate fencing strategies so you can offer competitive pricing while protecting your premium segments. Smart discounting is part of effective revenue management.

4.Will my rates be too high and scare away guests?

No. Revenue management is not about pricing yourself out of the market—it's about finding the optimal price point that maximizes revenue while maintaining occupancy. We carefully monitor competitor pricing, market positioning, and booking pace to ensure your rates remain competitive. During high-demand periods, we may price higher (because guests are willing to pay more), but during slower periods, we'll optimize rates to attract bookings. The result is usually increased occupancy at better average rates. We also use psychological pricing strategies and rate integrity practices to maintain your brand value while staying competitive.

5.How quickly will I see results?

Most hotels start seeing improvements within the first 30-60 days. Early wins often include better rate positioning, improved booking pace, and increased RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room). More substantial revenue growth becomes clear within 3-6 months as strategies mature and adapt to seasonal patterns. Properties with less sophisticated pricing typically see faster, more dramatic results, while those already doing some revenue management see steady optimization gains. We provide weekly and monthly performance reports so you can track progress from day one. The key is consistency—revenue management is a long-term strategy that compounds results over time.
